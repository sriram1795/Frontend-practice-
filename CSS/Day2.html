MY HOME TOWN

Narasapuram

Narasapuram is a coastal town and former trading port in the West Godavari district of Andhra Pradesh, historically significant for its shipbuilding, especially during the 18th and 19th centuries when teak was exported from there. Although no longer a major port, the tradition of small boat building continues, and the town retains its historical and cultural importance as a coastal area with natural beauty.

Nature’s Tunnel – Narasapuram

This picturesque tree-lined road in Narasapuram is known for its natural green canopy, offering travelers a refreshing escape from the hustle of town life. The stretch is a favorite among cyclists and bikers, giving a peaceful ride through the shade of age-old trees.

Narasapuram - Beach

Perupalem Beach, located near Narasapuram, is a serene and less-crowded spot on the Bay of Bengal coast, known for its blue waters, local cuisine, and accommodation options, offering a tranquil escape with a glimpse into coastal village life. You can reach the beach by first traveling to Narasapuram via train or bus and then taking local transport to the beach itself

Narasapuram - Godavari

Narsapuram, a town in Andhra Pradesh, is famous for its 19th-century crochet lace industry, which provides income for local women. It is also an economic hub for the surrounding agricultural region along the Vasista Godavari River . Historically, Narsapuram was an important Dutch trading port on the Coromandel Coast, with a strong shipbuilding tradition and serving as a gateway to the Godavari River.

Lace Park

Narsapuram Lace Park in Andhra Pradesh is a hub where skilled artisans create exquisite handcrafted crochet lace, a craft introduced by Irish missionaries in the 1800s. The park offers a dedicated space for artisans and holds a GI tag for its unique, intricate products like doilies, table runners, and fashion accessories. It is an important economic driver, supporting over a lakh women artisans in the region. The park also exports products worldwide, helping preserve and promote this traditional art form.
